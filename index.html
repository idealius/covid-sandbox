COVID-19 Notes

1. Many region's reporting agencies warn the most recent days are subject to change. (Guess: the last 3-17 days on average.) Typically, they fill in higher as time passes.

2. The regions with high peaks for cases and deaths early in the pandemic are not necessarily appropriate to compare to regions now because the overall testing was most likely lower, and the deaths were most likely higher, then.

3. The congruent seasonality of COVID-19 and other transmissable diseases isn't well understood.

Graph Notes

2. Ranking in the text area above is absolute for respective dataset (US or Global) and does not have the rolling average applied, though it is limited by the number of days selected.
